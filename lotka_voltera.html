Given that we some ocillations in the two populations we experimented with the lotka-volterra model with different parameters.

Oscillatory Dynamics

  1. Using a function that iterates through combinations of the parameters, stimulating the system for each set of parameters, checking for oscillations by analyzing the population trends, and returning the parameters that best fit oscillatory dynamics.

To try and make our model more representative of the actual complexity of the ecosystem, we want to take into consideration some of the real-world events that happened to these populations.

  1. Wolf immigration event (Inbreeding Relief): adding a one time population boost (1980-82)
  2. Disease in Wolf population (canine parovirus): drop in wolf population at a specific time (1996-97)
  3. Harsh winter on moose pop: sudden drop in moose population at another specific time (late 1990s)
  4. Ticks impact of moose pop: continuous negative effect on moose over a short time frame (2015-16)
